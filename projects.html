Research & Technical Projects

This page highlights selected academic and technical projects related to my research interests in CMOS-MEMS sensors, VLSI systems, and low-power circuit design.

CMOS-Compatible MEMS Sensor Interface Design

Designed and analyzed electronic readout circuits for CMOS-compatible MEMS sensors, focusing on low-noise amplification and power-efficient signal conditioning. The work involved circuit-level simulations and evaluation of noise–power trade-offs.

Tools: Cadence Virtuoso, LTspice

Low-Power Analog Front-End for Sensing Applications

Developed a low-power analog front-end architecture for sensor signal acquisition, emphasizing low-noise performance and energy efficiency. The project explored different biasing techniques and circuit topologies.

Tools: Cadence Virtuoso, MATLAB

VLSI System-Level Analysis for Integrated Sensors

Performed system-level analysis of integrated sensor architectures, studying the interaction between MEMS devices, analog front-end circuits, and digital processing blocks to optimize overall system performance.

Focus: Noise, Power, Scalability

Ongoing & Future Projects

Additional projects related to integrated sensing systems, advanced MEMS interfaces, and mixed-signal VLSI designs will be added as the research progresses.
