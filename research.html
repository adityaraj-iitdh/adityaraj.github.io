Research Overview

My research interests lie at the intersection of CMOS-compatible MEMS sensors, VLSI system design, and low-power analog and mixed-signal integrated circuits. I focus on developing efficient electronic interfaces for sensing applications, emphasizing performance, power efficiency, and scalability.

Research Interests

  • CMOS-MEMS Sensor Design and Integration
  • Analog & Mixed-Signal VLSI Circuits
  • Low-Noise and Low-Power Sensor Interfaces
  • Integrated Circuits for Sensing and Measurement Systems
  • Electronic Readout Circuits for MEMS Devices

Current Research

My current work involves the design and analysis of CMOS-based sensing architectures, with particular emphasis on integrating MEMS devices with low-power electronic readout circuits. The research explores trade-offs between noise performance, power consumption, and system-level integration.

Future Research Directions

In the future, I aim to extend my research toward fully integrated sensing systems, combining MEMS devices, analog front-end circuits, and digital processing blocks. The long-term goal is to develop scalable and energy-efficient sensor platforms for real-world applications.
